# LinkedIn Positioning Brief — Samuel Wong

Prepared for the Copywriter. Read this before writing a single word.

---

## 1. Target Audience

**Primary: SME founders and senior leaders (CEOs, CMOs, CTOs) who have data but aren't using it well.**
These are founders of 50-500 person companies in Australia (expanding to APAC). They've got data sitting in spreadsheets or siloed tools. They know they *should* be using it but don't have a full-time Head of Data. They're not looking for a "data consultant" — they want someone who can step in, fix the mess, and get out. They search for "fractional CTO" or "data strategy" or "analytics consultant" on LinkedIn.

**Secondary: Data professionals looking for mentorship or career guidance.**
Early-to-mid career analysts in Australia who've seen his talks, his mentoring track record, or his Onalytics ranking. They follow him for signal, not noise. This audience builds his authority and creates inbound opportunities (speaking, workshops, advisory).

**Tertiary: Potential speaking and advisory clients.**
Event organisers, conference committees, and companies looking for fractional/executive-level data leadership. They Google him, land on LinkedIn, and need to see credibility in 5 seconds.

---

## 2. Positioning Statement

**Samuel helps SME founders turn their data chaos into growth levers — as their fractional Head of Data, without the full-time price tag.**

He's not a "data consultant." He's a founder who's spent 12 years inside the data trenches at Crown, Flybuys, and 10+ SMEs, and now brings that enterprise-grade rigour to companies that can't afford (or don't need) a full-time analytics team. The startup energy comes from the fact that he IS a founder — he runs his own business, he's not cosplaying as one.

## 3. Key Differentiators

These are the four things Samuel has that 95% of "data consultants" on LinkedIn do NOT. The copywriter should find ways to weave all four into the profile without listing them as bullet points.

### 3.1 Fractional, Not Freelance
He doesn't do one-off dashboard builds. He steps in as the strategic data leader — setting direction, building capability, then handing it off. This is a fundamentally different engagement model from "hire me to write SQL." The Framing: he's the interim Head of Data your company needs but isn't ready to hire full-time.

### 3.2 Scale Credibility, SME Empathy
He's run analytics at Crown Resorts (multi-property, regulated, C-suite) and Flybuys (9M+ members). That's not a line on a resume — it's proof he's seen what "scaled data" actually looks like. But he chooses to work with SMEs because that's where the leverage is. He brings the playbook without the bureaucracy.

### 3.3 The Mentoring Thread
10+ mentees across Melbourne University, UNSW, RMIT, Monash, and the Data Visualisation Society. This is not a side hobby — it's a signal that he genuinely cares about data culture, not just selling hours. It also creates a network effect: mentees become advocates, referrers, and future collaborators. The copywriter should make this feel organic, not like a CSR initiative.

### 3.4 Onalytics Top 33 Global Big Data Influencer
This is a credibility stamp that most consultants would kill for. It should be present but not shouting. One clean mention, not a recurring theme.

## 4. Tone & Voice

### Sound like:
- A founder talking to other founders. Not a consultant pitching a client.
- Someone who's done the work and doesn't need to oversell it.
- Direct, practical, and slightly irreverent. He's seen what doesn't work at scale and has opinions about it.
- Confident but generous — he shares what he knows rather than gatekeeping it.

### Do NOT sound like:
- A corporate LinkedIn thought leader ("Excited to announce...", "Thrilled to share...")
- A generic data consultant ("I help organisations unlock the power of their data...")
- A job applicant listing competencies
- A résumé converted to profile format

### Specific guidance:
- Use first person. Short sentences. No jargon soup.
- Numbers should feel like proof points, not bragging. "9M+ members" hits differently when it's in a sentence about a specific problem solved, not a standalone flex.
- The word "fractional" should feel natural, not buzzy. It's his model, not a marketing gimmick.
- Avoid: "passionate", "results-driven", "synergy", "ecosystem" (unless referring to Flybuys literally), "leveraging", "unlocking", "赋能" (unless the audience specifically requests Cantonese).

## 6. Profile Structure Recommendations

### Headline
**Current state (assumed):** Likely something generic like "Founder at datafying" or "Senior Data Leader."
**Recommendation:** Lead with what he does for people, not his title. Something like:
> Founder @ datafying | Fractional Head of Data & AI for SMEs | Turning data chaos into growth levers

Keep it under 220 characters. The word "Fractional" is the hook — it signals a specific engagement model.

### About Section
**Lead with the problem he solves**, not his bio. Start with a founder's pain point (e.g., "You've got dashboards nobody uses and a data team that's underwater") and position himself as the solution. Then layer in credibility (Crown, Flybuys, scale). End with what working with him looks like — not a call to action, but a picture of the outcome.

**Length:** 3-4 short paragraphs. No wall of text. Each paragraph should do ONE job:
1. Problem/opener
2. What he does (fractional model)
3. Proof (corporate + SME track record)
4. The mentoring/culture thread + soft CTA

### Experience Section
**datafying (current):** This should be the longest and most detailed. It's his present. Emphasise the breadth of SME clients and the types of problems he solves. Each bullet should answer "so what?" — not just what he did, but what changed.

**Crown Resorts:** Keep it tight. 3 bullets max. Focus on the CX transformation and the $500M investment strategy he informed — that's the headline. The team leadership is implied.

**Flybuys (both roles):** Consolidate if LinkedIn allows, or treat as one narrative. The 9M+ member scale and the retail media revenue uplift are the standouts. Don't repeat metrics across roles.

**What to cut:** Remove any bullet that just describes a tool or process without an outcome. "Built dashboards in Tableau" means nothing. "Built dashboards that reduced reporting time by 60%" means something.

### Skills & Endorsements
**Front-load:** Data Strategy, Customer Analytics, Marketing Analytics, SQL, Python, dbt, Snowflake, Customer Segmentation, Executive Storytelling, Data Mentoring.
**Remove or deprioritise:** Generic skills like "Microsoft Office", "PowerPoint", "Agile Methodologies" — they dilute the signal.

### Featured Section
Should include: any published articles, speaking engagements, or case studies. If he doesn't have these yet, the Copywriter should note it as a gap to fill.

## 7. Competitive Framing

There are roughly three types of "data" profiles on LinkedIn. Samuel needs to be clearly differentiated from all of them.

### Type 1: The Corporate Data Director
Ex-Deloitte, ex-McKinsey, now "independent consultant." Talks about "digital transformation" and "data-driven culture." Their profile reads like a partner bio page.

**How Samuel is different:** He's not from a consulting firm. He's been IN the organisations — Crown, Flybuys — doing the work, not presenting the slides. And he chose SMEs. Frame this as deliberate, not a fallback.

### Type 2: The Freelance Analyst
"I build dashboards and write SQL." Rates on their profile. Portfolio of Tableau vizzes. Transactional, project-based.

**How Samuel is different:** He's not selling hours or deliverables. He's selling outcomes and strategic direction. The fractional model means he's embedded, not outsourced. This distinction matters because it attracts founders who want a partner, not a vendor.

### Type 3: The Thought Leader
Posts 3 times a day. Hot takes on AI. 50K followers. Never actually done the work at a company.

**How Samuel is different:** He has the thought leadership (Onalytics ranking, mentoring, speaking) PLUS the track record. He doesn't need to post 3 times a day because his work speaks. His profile should feel earned, not performed.

### The Positioning Line
**Samuel is the data leader who's been in the room where the decisions get made — and now he brings that to companies that don't have the room yet.**

## Summary for the Copywriter

Your job: Write a LinkedIn profile that makes a founder think "that's exactly my problem" within 5 seconds of landing on the page, and makes a data professional think "I want to learn from this person" within 10.

- Start with the problem, not the bio.
- Use numbers as proof, not decoration.
- Keep the voice human. If it sounds like it was written by a recruiter, delete it and start over.
- Every section should earn its space. If a bullet doesn't change how someone sees Samuel, cut it.
