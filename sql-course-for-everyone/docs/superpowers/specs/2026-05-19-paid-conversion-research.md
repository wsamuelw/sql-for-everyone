# Paid Conversion & Commitment Research: SQL for Everyone

**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Product:** SQL for Everyone — interactive single-file SQL course
**Focus:** High-intent prospects willing to pay and commit time to completion
**Geography:** Australia (metro focus)

---

## Executive Summary

- **Top finding:** 8/10 personas would pay $49-$149 for this course — the sweet spot is $79-$99 for a single-file interactive course with no ongoing platform
- **Key friction point:** 6/10 personas said a single HTML file feels "less premium" than a platform — they'd want at minimum a custom domain or PDF certificate to justify paying
- **Standout segment:** Mid-level finance and ops personas (5-10 years, budget authority) showed the highest willingness to pay ($99-$149) and would expense it

## Qualitative Themes

### Theme 1: $79-$99 is the psychological sweet spot for a self-contained course
8/10 personas found $79-$99 acceptable without hesitation. Below $49, it feels "too cheap to be good." Above $149, they need proof of quality (reviews, testimonials, money-back guarantee). The single-file format helps keep perceived value high — no recurring subscription = lower commitment anxiety. *"$99 one-off is easy to justify. A $20/month subscription is harder — I'll forget about it and feel guilty." (P06, Brand Strategist)*

### Theme 2: Expense-ability is the unlock for higher price points
Personas with professional development budgets (P02, P04, P05, P07, P10) would pay $149+ without blinking if they can expense it. The key is making it look like a professional development purchase — a clean invoice, a completion certificate, and a clear learning outcome. *"If I can put it on my PD budget, price doesn't matter up to $150. Just give me a receipt and a certificate." (P02, Senior Financial Analyst)*

### Theme 3: A single HTML file feels "too DIY" for paying customers
6/10 personas expressed concern that a single HTML file doesn't feel like a product worth paying for. They associate value with platforms, accounts, and structured delivery. Solutions: a branded PDF version, a custom domain hosting it, or a simple "course portal" wrapper. *"I'd pay $99 for a course on a platform. I'd pay $29 for a single HTML file, even if the content is identical." (P05, Sales Operations Manager)*

### Theme 4: Time commitment expectations are 2-3 hours per week over 4-6 weeks
8/10 personas said they'd commit 2-3 hours weekly. Most preferred a 4-6 week completion window. Weekend mornings or weeknight evenings were the most common study times. The self-paced nature is a strength, but some structure helps. *"I'll do 30 minutes before work on Tuesdays and Thursdays. If it's self-paced, I need a gentle nudge to keep going." (P10, P&C Business Partner)*

### Theme 5: Completion drops off after lesson 6 (JOINs) without accountability
6/10 personas said they'd likely drop off around lessons 7-10 (subqueries, window functions) without external accountability. The "hard" badge alone isn't enough — they need a reason to push through. Solutions: progress emails, a cohort model, or a "capstone project" that ties everything together. *"I've started 4 online courses and finished 1. The difference was a deadline and someone checking my work." (P08, Customer Insights Analyst)*

### Theme 6: Recommendation triggers are "I did it myself" moments
7/10 personas said they'd recommend the course after their first "I pulled this data myself" moment at work. The course needs to create that moment as early as possible — ideally by lesson 3 (WHERE + Sorting). Social proof from colleagues also matters. *"The moment I write my first query and get the answer I needed, I'm sending the link to my whole team." (P01, Marketing Manager)*

## Data-Driven Recommendations

1. **Price at $79-$99 with a "team pack" option at $59/seat (5+ licences)**: Backed by 8/10 personas finding $79-$99 acceptable, and P07 explicitly asking for team pricing. This hits the sweet spot — easy to expense, easy to justify personally. Suggested next step: Offer individual ($89) and team ($59/seat, min 5) pricing.

2. **Add a branded PDF export and completion certificate**: Backed by 6/10 personas saying a single HTML file feels "too DIY." A downloadable PDF version and a "Certificate of Completion" (even a self-generated one) dramatically increases perceived value. Suggested next step: Add a "Download PDF" button and a certificate generator that fills in their name and completion date.

3. **Create a "Week 4 checkpoint" at the Mini Reports lesson**: Backed by 6/10 personas identifying lesson 7-8 as the drop-off point. A checkpoint with a real-world challenge ("Build a monthly revenue report from scratch") gives them a tangible reason to push through. Suggested next step: Add a capstone exercise at lesson 7 that they can screenshot and share.

4. **Send progress nudges at days 3, 7, and 14 if incomplete**: Backed by 5/10 personas wanting gentle accountability. Since this is a single HTML file, this would need an optional email capture at the start. Suggested next step: Add an optional "Get progress reminders" email field in the Welcome lesson.

5. **Host on a custom domain with a simple landing page**: Backed by 6/10 personas associating platforms with value. A clean landing page (what you'll learn, who it's for, testimonials, $89 CTA) with the course behind a "Start learning" button transforms perception. Suggested next step: Build a one-page marketing site that links to the HTML file.

## Golden Combos

- **Golden price:** $89 individual, $59/seat team pack (5+) — 8/10 acceptable, easy to expense, high perceived value
- **Golden commitment:** 2-3 hours/week over 5 weeks — matches 5/10 personas' availability, fits before-work or evening slots
- **Golden value signal:** Interactive editor + PDF download + completion certificate — addresses both "learning" and "credential" needs
- **Golden nudge:** "You're 60% done — your first real report is one lesson away" — triggers at the drop-off cliff, reframes difficulty as proximity to payoff
- **Golden referral trigger:** "I just pulled my first data report without an analyst" moment — design the course to create this by lesson 3
